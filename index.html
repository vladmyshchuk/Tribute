Martin Scorsese

The Godfather of Gangster Movies

The life of the legendary director at a glance:

  • 1942 - Born in Flushing, New York.
  • 1950 - Due to financial hardship, the Scorsese family are forced to move from their suburb in Queens to Manhattan's Little Italy neighborhood.
  • 1953 - Scorsese's father, who is a huge film fan, takes him to see The Magic Box, a British film about the history of cinema. Immediately Scorsese falls in love with cinema and decides to be either an actor or painter when he grows up.
  • 1960 - Scorsese enrolls at NYU to study filmmaking.
  • 1964 - Scorsese graduates from NYU.
  • 1965 - Scorsese gets married to Laraine Marie Brennan in NY. After about a year their daughter Catherine is born. Not long after the couple end their marriage by divorce.
  • 1965 - Scorsese receives his masters in fine arts from NYU.
  • 1967 - Scorsese produces his first feature film, Who's That Knocking At My Door? starring a local actor named Harvey Keitel. Like many of his later works, the film was about an Italian-American young man growing up on the rough streets of New York City. Scorsese's film has caught the interest of director Roger Corman, who becomes his mentor.
  • 1973 - Scorsese releases his first masterpiece, in my opinion, Mean Streets, a bold, violent Italian-American drama also set in New York City. This film also starred Keitel as well as a young actor name Robert De Niro. De Niro was a hit and would go on to star in many more of Scorsese's films.
  • 1974 - Scorsese produces the drama Alice Doesn't Live Here Anymore. The actress Ellen Burstyn ends up winning an Oscar for best actress.
  • 1975 Scorsese and writer Julia B. Cameron are married. The couple has a daughter but again the marriage only lasts two years.
  • 1976 - Scorsese releases his 2nd masterpiece, Taxi Driver, a dark, psychological story of a man descending into madness. Robert DeNiro's performance as the insane Travic Bickle becomes legendary.
  • 1977 - Scorsese goes thru a dark period in his life due to his film New York, New York being a flop. He spirals into depression and cocaine addiction, leading him to check into a hospital for recovery.
  • 1979 Scorsese marries the actress Isabella Rossellini. The marriage comes to an end two years later.
  • 1980 - Scorsese and De Niro team up yet again to create a masterpiece of a film, Raging Bull. In the biopic, De Niro stars as the boxer Jake La Motta. Scorsese's longtime editor Thelma Schoonmaker wins Oscars for their own. Scorsese is nominated for Best Director but ends up losing.
  • 1983 - Scorsese creates the comedy film The King of Comedy, starring De Niro and Jerry Lewis. Unfortunately the movies flops at the box office but this did not stop Scorsese from getting right back to work on his next film.
  • 1985 - Scorsese marries Barbara De Fina, a producer who later collaborates with Scorsese on the films Goodfellas and Kundun. They end up divorcing in 1991.
  • 1986 - Scorsese releases the film The Color of Money, about a hustler played by Paul Newman. It ends up being one of his first big commercial successes with a $50 million domestic gross.
  • 1988 - Scorsese releases the film The Last Temptation of Christ, based on the Nikos Kazantzakis novel. The film recasts Jesus as a human rather than a divine figure which caused much controversy. Critics praised the film but religious groups obviously protested it. One group even went as far as to fire-bomb a theater in Paris showing the film.
  • 1990 - Scorsese releases one of his most iconic movies Goodfellas, set in his childhood neighborhood of Little Italy. Scorsese is nominated for Best Director Oscar but loses for the third time.
  • 1991 - Scorsese releases his horror film Cape Fear. It ends up becoming Scorsese's most commercially successful film to this point (approximately $80 million in gross domestic box office).
  • 1993 - Scorsese decides to take a break from his usual violent, urban setting of his films and releases The Age of Innocence. Scorsese was nominated again for an Oscar but did not win.
  • 1995 - Scorsese releases the film Casino, reuniting Goodfellas stars Robert De Niro and Joe Pesci. He also ends up creating a four-hour documentary about American cinema called A Personal Journey With Martin Scorsese Through American Movies.
  • 1997 - Scorsese produces Kundun about the early life of the Dalai Lama. The film release caused protests in China. The American Film Institute rewards Scorsese with its Life Achievement Award.
  • 1999 - Scorsese marries his fifth wife, Helen Morris, and have a daughter that same year.
  • 2002 - Scorsese releases the gangster epic Gangs of New York, a look at the violent inter-ethnic clashes in New York City in the 19th century. It was the first Scorsese film with actor Leonardo DiCaprio, who was to become one of his muses in the way of Robert De Niro and Harvey Keitel. Shockingly, the film was nominated for ten Oscars but failed to win one.
  • 2004 - Scorsese releases The Aviator,an epic biopic of the eccentric millionaire Howard Hughes. Starring Leonardo DiCaprio the film was a hit (over $100 million in gross domestic box office). Scorsese is nominated for his sixth Best Director Oscar and loses for the sixth time.
  • 2005 - Scorsese earns an Emmy for Outstanding Directing on the HBO series Boardwalk Empire.
  • 2007 - Scorsese finally wins his first Academy Award for Best Director for the film The Departed which starred DeCaprio, Jack Nicholson, and Matt Damon.
  • 2010 - Scorsese continues to work with DiCaprio to produce the psychological thriller Shutter Island.
  • 2011 - Scorsese releases a 3D historical adventure drama film Hugo. The film was nominated for 11 Academy Awards, winning five of them and becoming tied with the film The Artist for the most Academy Awards won by a single film in 2011.
  • 2013 - Scorsese and DiCaprio link up again to create an American biographical black comedy The Wolf of Wall Street. DiCaprio won the award for Best Actor-Motion Picture Musical or Comedy at the 2014 Golden Globe Awards, with the film being nominated for Best Motion Picture as well.
  • 2016 Scorsese releases a long-time passion project he had been working on for the past 25 years with the film Silence. The film received an Academy Award nomination for Best Cinematography.
  • 2019 - Scorsese is currently working on finishing the crime thriller drama The Irishman, starring Robert De Niro, Al Pacino, and Joe Pesci.
